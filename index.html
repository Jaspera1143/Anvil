DIY Guide: How to Claim Yourself

You can make a claim directly to your bank and the Financial Ombudsman Service for free. Here's how:

  1. Contact your bank first - Explain you were scammed and ask for a refund under the Contingent Reimbursement Model Code.
  2. Get a final response - Your bank has 8 weeks to investigate and give you a final decision.

Unique Property Investment Group - Potential Fraud

We've seen an increase in reports about property investment schemes promising unrealistic returns.

Warning Signs:

  • Guaranteed high returns (15%+)
  • Pressure to invest quickly
  • Unregulated companies
  • No FCA registration
  • Luxury lifestyle marketing

How to Recover:

If you've lost money, contact us immediately. Time limits apply for bank claims. The Financial Ombudsman typically requires claims within 6 years of the incident.

Crypto Scams on the Rise in 2026

Cryptocurrency scams have increased by 40% in the last year. New tactics include fake exchanges, celebrity endorsements, and "pig butchering" romance scams.

Common Crypto Scams:

  • Pump and dump schemes

Bank Transfer Scams: Your Rights

Under the Contingent Reimbursement Model Code, most banks must reimburse APP scam victims up to £85,000.

When Banks Must Refund:

  • You're a vulnerable customer
  • Bank ignored warning signs
  • You reported promptly
  • Scam was sophisticated

When They Might Not:

  • You ignored bank warnings
  • Gross negligence
  • Delayed reporting

APP Scams Explained

Authorised Push Payment (APP) scams occur when you're tricked into sending money to a fraudster posing as a legitimate person or organisation.

Under new rules, most banks must reimburse victims of APP scams up to £85,000.

Common types include:

  • Fake HMRC/Police calls
  • Impersonation of banks
  • Purchase scams
  • Investment fraud
  • Romance scams
